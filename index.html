The Dangers of Global Warming

Rising Sea Level

As global temperatures increase, glaciers and ice caps melt, causing sea levels to rise. This can lead to flooding in low-lying areas and the displacement of millions of people who live in coastal regions.

More frequent and intense natural disasters

Climate change can lead to more frequent and severe natural disasters such as hurricanes, tornadoes, droughts, and wildfires. These events can cause significant damage to infrastructure, property, and human life.

Food and water shortages

Global warming can disrupt agriculture, leading to food shortages and higher prices for basic necessities. It can also cause water scarcity, particularly in regions that rely on glaciers and snowpack for their water supply. This can lead to conflicts over resources and humanitarian crises.
